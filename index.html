EMMA KATE COLEMAN

the next five years

an introduction

My name is Emma. To my sister, I'm Emma Kate. To my father, I'm Em. I attend a liberal arts college in Lexington, Virginia. I study photography and journalism.

My studies encourage me to look beyond, and to look deep. Photography forces me to see beyond the everyday and to notice light, color and movement. Because of photography, I am conscious of time and space. Journalism forces me to see deeply into the lives of my neighbors, leaders and friends. Because of journalism, I am conscious of people's stories.

Who I am today is a culmination of Emma's gone. I am the product of their loves and joys, as well as their sadnesses and pains.

looking back

As an undergraduate student, I have felt overwhelming doubt, loss and misdirection. I knew I liked photography and journalism, but I was scared that my talents would never be enough for the world outside of Lexington. I worried constantly that I wouldn't get a job as a journalist when I graduated, or that I would never be hired as a portrait photographer.

These worries and fears were exacerbated by the people I chose to spend my time with. The individual I dated for the majority of my undergraduate experience was manipulative and emotionally abusive. His unhealthy need to control my every step, and my unhealthy need to please the people I loved, made for diminished dreams. At his bidding, I quit sports I loved, abandonned friends I cherished and ran from job opportunities I wanted. I became someone else. I was no longer Emma. Not Emma Kate. Not Em.

When the toxic relationship was terminated, I found myself more lost than ever before. I was starting from scratch. With my past dreams and interests thrown out the window, I scrambled to find new ones. I began the process of finding Emma again. I returned to the gym. I picked up my camera. I started writing. I am returning.

looking forward

I have since found my confidence again. My dreams, while not the same, are back and they are vivid. I regularly take self-portraits, putting myself in the spotlight, reminding myself that I am of worth, that my needs are important, that my talents and dreams and passions are legitimate.

In the next five years, I trust my dreams will change yet again. I no longer want to write for National Geographic. I want to write for small town papers. I no longer want to photograph for National Geographic. I want to photograph the people closest to me, for the people closest to me. In five years, I trust that I will want to write and photograph with new intentions. But those intentions will be mine, and no one else's.

My future will be dominated by self-discovery and examination. I will become the most "Emma" I can be. And she will be beautiful. And powerful. And strong. And intelligent. And talented. And worthy. And kind. That is all I need my next five years to be. I don't need to marry, or get that perfect post-grad job. I need to be myself. The dreams will follow.

Here's to the next five years.
